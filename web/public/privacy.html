Privacy Policy

GitPhotos is built so that we never see your data. This page explains what that means in plain terms.

Last updated: 22 June 2026

The short version

There is no GitPhotos server. Your photos and your GitHub access token never reach us. Photos go only to the private GitHub repositories in the account you sign into, and your token stays in the iOS Keychain on your device.

#Who we are

GitPhotos (“the app”, “we”) is an independent iOS application that backs up your photo library to your own private GitHub repositories. This policy describes how the app handles information.

#Information we collect

We do not collect any personal data. GitPhotos has no backend servers, no analytics, no advertising SDKs, and no tracking. We do not create an account for you, and we have no database in which any information about you could be stored.

Specifically, the following never reach us:

  • Your photos and videos — read from your device only to upload them to your GitHub.
  • Your GitHub access token — stored solely in the iOS Keychain on your device.
  • Your GitHub username, email, or repository contents.
  • Usage, diagnostic, or location data.

#How your photos are handled

When you back up a photo, the app reads it from your device’s photo library and sends it directly to the GitHub API, where it is committed to a private repository on the GitHub account you signed into. The connection uses standard HTTPS encryption.

The data flows from your phone to GitHub and nowhere else. We are not an intermediary in that transfer — there is no GitPhotos service in the path.

#Your GitHub token

To act on your behalf, GitPhotos needs a GitHub credential — either obtained through GitHub’s device authorization flow or a personal access token you provide. This credential is stored only in the iOS Keychain, a secure, encrypted store managed by your device. It is never transmitted to us and never leaves your device except when authenticating directly with GitHub.

#Photo library permission

GitPhotos requests access to your photo library (NSPhotoLibraryUsageDescription) for one reason: to read the photos you choose to back up. You can grant access to all photos or only selected items, and you can change or revoke this permission at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Photos.

#Third-party services

GitPhotos relies on GitHub to store your photos. When you use the app, your interactions with GitHub are governed by GitHub’s own terms and privacy practices:

  • GitHub Privacy Statement
  • GitHub Terms of Service

GitPhotos is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or sponsored by GitHub, Inc.

#Children’s privacy

GitPhotos is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect information from anyone, regardless of age. (As noted above, the app collects no personal information at all.)

#Your control and choices

Because everything lives in your own GitHub account and on your own device, you remain fully in control:

  • Delete your backups by deleting the relevant repositories on GitHub.
  • Revoke the app’s access by deleting the personal access token in your GitHub settings, or by signing out in the app.
  • Remove all local data by deleting the app, which clears its Keychain entry and cached images from your device.

#Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update the date at the top of this page and post the revised version here.
